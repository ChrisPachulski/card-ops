
# Advanced Techniques

Expert-level strategies used in the credit card optimization community. These evaluations should be aware of them and mention when relevant, but not build scoring around them -- they carry elevated risk or require specific circumstances. For the issuer rules these techniques interact with (5/24, once-per-lifetime, etc.), see [[issuer-rules]]. For bureau management and application spacing context, see [[application-timing]].

## Credit Freeze / Bureau Lock Strategy

Freezing specific credit bureaus forces issuers to pull from a bureau of your choice, managing inquiry distribution. Requires unfreezing before applying to an issuer that pulls from the frozen bureau. Use Experian, TransUnion, or Equifax freeze portals (all free). Strategy: freeze the bureau with the most inquiries, apply to issuers that pull from your cleanest bureau.

## Authorized User (AU) Strategy

Adding/removing authorized users on another person's card can affect credit profile. Being added as AU on an old, high-limit card boosts AAoA and reduces utilization. AU accounts from most issuers count toward 5/24 but can be disputed off. P2 (partner/player 2) coordination enables household-level portfolio optimization. [WEB] DoC, TPG

## Amex NLL (No Lifetime Language) Offers

Some targeted or incognito Amex offers lack the standard "once per lifetime" restriction, allowing repeat bonuses on the same card. Found via: incognito browser, referral links, CardMatch, direct mailers. These are not guaranteed and Amex may change their availability at any time. [WEB] DoC

## In-Branch Pre-Approvals

Some Chase branches offer pre-approvals that bypass 5/24. Not available online, requires visiting a branch and checking with a banker. Capital One pre-qualified mailers may also bypass normal rules. These are opportunistic, not plannable. [WEB] DoC, Freq. Miler

## Gardening Periods

Planned inactivity (3-6 months with no new applications) to let inquiries age and profile season before targeting inquiry-sensitive issuers like US Bank or Barclays. Strategy: aggressive application period -> garden -> target sensitive issuers.
